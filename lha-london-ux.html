LHA London Room Listings

Transforming a high-friction, "blind" enquiry process into a fully browsable, filterable booking experience that drastically reduced operational bloat.

The Brief

LHA London, a charity running 14 hostels across the capital, relied on a single generic enquiry form. With no way to browse rooms or check availability, the system generated high volumes of mismatched leads. The brief was to overhaul the discovery and booking flow to better serve users and staff.

My Role

End-to-end delivery: UX Research, UI Design, Wireframing, Custom WordPress Development (PHP & ACF), and system architecture.

The Problem

UX · Problem Discovery

The existing user journey was effectively a black box. The LHA team was buried in single-room enquiries they couldn’t fulfil, while prospective tenants had no upfront visibility into what rooms looked like, what they cost, or if they were even available.

This friction was costing the LHA staff significant operational time in back-and-forth emails, and leaving applicants with a frustrating first impression of the charity. The goal was to build a system that accurately managed expectations *before* a user ever reached a form.

The redesigned master search page featuring live availability, photos, and extensive filtering.

Research & Discovery

Competitor Audit · UX Strategy

Before designing the new interface, I audited direct and indirect competitors across the student accommodation and co-living sectors. I needed to identify the UX patterns users already trusted, and pinpoint exactly where LHA's current experience was falling short.

Key Insights

  • Availability is scanned first: Users look for availability status before price or photos. Surfacing this immediately removes the largest source of wasted enquiries.
  • Photos drive intent: Room imagery is the strongest metric for conversion. Text-only listings significantly underperformed.
  • Waitlists retain leads: When a room is full, providing a clear path to a waiting list keeps the applicant engaged rather than bouncing them entirely.
  • Filters self-qualify: Empowering users to sort by hostel, price, and type naturally filters out mismatched leads before they hit the team's inbox.

Auditing co-living and student accommodation platforms to establish UX benchmarks.

System Logic & Solution

System Architecture · UI Design

I designed the shift from a generic, contextless form to a rich, browsable listing experience. I established a clear room card format—prioritizing photos, price, room type, and a live availability badge.

Smart Routing & Pre-population

One of the most impactful UX decisions was the introduction of dynamic CTA logic. If a room is marked 'Available', the user is routed to the Booking Form. If it's 'Full', they are routed to a Waiting List Form.

To eliminate user fatigue and ensure data cleanliness, I built a system that passes URL parameters from the room card directly into the form. The user arrives with the Room Name, Hostel, and Room Type already pre-populated. The LHA team now receives perfectly structured, actionable data with every single submission.

Mapping the intelligent user flow: utilizing URL parameters to auto-populate forms based on live room status.

Impact & Development

Development · Outcomes

To make this scalable, I custom-built an Advanced Custom Fields (ACF) backend interface in WordPress. The LHA team can now independently update pricing, toggle availability, and manage inventory without ever needing to submit a developer request.

HigherQuality Enquiries
CleanerStructured Form Data
100%CMS Independence

By surfacing availability upfront and introducing smart form routing, the volume of mismatched, unfulfillable requests plummeted. The project successfully aligned user expectations with LHA's business realities, saving countless hours of administrative work.
